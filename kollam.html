Ashtamudi Lake

The Eight-Armed Backwater Paradise

The Ancient Port of Trade

Known historically as Quilon, Kollam is one of the oldest ports in the Arabian Sea. It is the southern gateway to the backwaters of Kerala and is world-famous for its thriving cashew industry.

With the scenic Ashtamudi Lake covering nearly 30% of the district, Kollam offers a tranquil escape through mangrove forests, traditional fishing villages, and the legendary Munroe Island.
