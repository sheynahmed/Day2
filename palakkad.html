Palakkad

The Land of Palmyra Trees and Paddy Fields

Malampuzha

The Vrindavan of Kerala

Gateway to the Ghats

Palakkad is a vast expanse of fertile plains and mountains, lying at the foot of the Western Ghats. It is famous for the Palakkad Gap, a natural break in the mountain range that connects Kerala to the plains of Tamil Nadu.

Known for its unique culture, traditional music, and the historic Palakkad Fort built by Hyder Ali, this district is a paradise for nature lovers and history buffs alike. It is also home to the Silent Valley National Park, an untouched tropical evergreen forest.
