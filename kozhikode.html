Where History Met the World

Kozhikode, formerly known as Calicut, is where Vasco da Gama first stepped onto Indian soil at Kappad Beach in 1498. For centuries, it was the capital of the powerful Zamorins and a major global hub for the spice trade.

Today, Kozhikode is equally famous for its warm hospitality and its legendary cuisine—from the world-famous Kozhikode Biryani to the sweet Halwa of S.M. Street. It is a city where history, commerce, and culture coexist beautifully.

Iconic Kozhikode Landmarks

S.M. Street (Mittai Theruvu)

The busiest street in the city, famous for sweets, textiles, and old-world charm.

Thusharagiri Falls

A stunning three-stage waterfall perfect for trekking and nature lovers.

Beypore Port & Uru Making

An ancient port famous for building massive traditional wooden ships called 'Uru'.
